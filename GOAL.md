# ALife Emergence and Adaptive Pressure Goal

## Goal-slot statement

Build a bounded, reproducible ALife research track that uses literal high-dimensional Life to measure when geometry becomes averaging and high-dimensional cell state on nontrivial bounded-degree topology to discover predictive mathematics of emergence; turn the resulting worlds into graded discovery episodes, and accept outputs only when they produce compute-matched regime-coverage expansion or a verified gain over the existing ontology registry for downstream consumers such as They Sing and Confinement Width.

## Purpose

The track studies the mapping

\[
\text{local mechanics} + \text{initial conditions} + \text{interaction geometry} + \text{noise}
\longrightarrow
\text{macroscopic organization}.
\]

Its scientific product is not attractive simulation output. It is reusable knowledge: phase maps, predictive macroscopic variables, invariants, causal mechanisms, control laws, diverse adaptive pressures, and investigation traces showing how a claim was or was not earned.

The track is a horizontal capability for other programs, not a storyworld and not initially a separate vertical. It supplies environments, adapted occupants, quality-diversity archives, mathematical descriptions, and graded investigation episodes.

## Substrate split

### Geometry-to-averaging control

Use literal binary Life-like cellular automata on high-dimensional lattices as a control. Factor lattice dimension separately from neighborhood degree so that geometric dimension is not confused with increased averaging.

Measure at minimum:

- Accuracy of density-only or mean-field predictors.
- Pair and higher-order spatial correlations.
- Correlation length and information-propagation range.
- Persistent localized structures and their lifetimes.
- Regime boundaries, finite-size effects, and compute cost.

The intended result is a phase map locating when local combinatorial geometry ceases to add explanatory value beyond averaged density.

### Mathematical-discovery laboratory

Use vector-valued cells on bounded-degree fixed, rewired, or coevolving graphs. Keep local update rules explicit and instrumentable; use edge-conditioned transport maps where connection, curvature, or holonomy quantities are claimed.

Search for:

- Order parameters and phase transitions.
- Spectral predictors and transport invariants.
- Conservation laws and symmetry classes.
- Coherent structures, defects, and boundaries.
- Perturbation repair, morphogenesis, and path dependence.
- Causal load-bearing nodes, edges, states, and mechanisms under registered targets and horizons.
- Coarse-grained models that predict unseen seeds, sizes, rules, and topologies.

## Epistemic ladder

Represent each discovery task on two independent axes:

1. **Oracle availability:** simulator oracle present or absent.
2. **Identification:** point-identified or set-identified.

The Level 4/5 boundary changes point targets into set-valued targets. Level 5 remains trainable through set coverage, sharpness, set distance, held-out prediction, intervention sufficiency, and compression-relative-to-competitors. The Level 5/6 boundary removes the simulator oracle; beyond it, process evidence must not be presented as a truth label.

Bootstrap Levels 1-4 with CA families containing planted or analytically known effects, nulls, conserved quantities, reachability constraints, and causal influence structures. Create Level 5 by masking observations or permitting multiple valid coarse-grainings. Treat oracle-absent Level 6 as transfer evaluation unless an external task supplies independently valid labels.

## Registration and abstention

Before seeds are drawn, every investigator registers:

- Target quantity or identified set.
- Smallest effect of practical interest.
- Required precision or set sharpness.
- Confidence or error target.
- Experimental design and intervention semantics.
- Compute budget and stopping rule.
- Forecast that the design will reach the target within budget.

Score the registration forecast for calibration as well as scoring the final claim. Reward abstention only when an oracle-backed identification certificate or registered reference-design envelope shows that the target was unreachable within budget. A competing admissible investigator reaching the target makes abstention avoidable. Failure by all sampled competitors is not proof of impossibility; unresolved episodes are censored rather than labeled correct abstentions.

## Two separate discovery ledgers

Do not use one scalar called novelty for two different achievements.

### Generator-coverage archive

Record world configurations, behavior descriptors, regimes, search budgets, seeds, persistence, and downstream evaluation effects. **Coverage expansion** means reaching a persistent regime outside the compute-matched baseline generator archive.

### Ontology registry

Record every accepted macroscopic variable with its definition, units, symmetry or equivalence class, estimator, valid domain, invariances, predictive targets, intervention tests, known confounds, and failures. **Ontology gain** means that a non-equivalent variable improves a registered held-out prediction, intervention, or compression objective at matched complexity and survives fresh-world confirmation.

The seed measurement vocabulary is an input. The discovered vocabulary and justified registry amendments are outputs.

## Discovery-episode product

Retain each graded episode as a tuple containing:

\[
(\text{world},\ \text{hidden mechanics},\ \text{visible observations},\ \text{registration},\
\text{investigation trace},\ \text{claim or abstention},\ \text{oracle evidence},\ \text{score}).
\]

Score false causal claims, correct exposure diagnosis, effect or set recovery, predictive calibration, registered abstention, holdout transfer, reproducibility, and compute spent per defensible claim. Never treat ticks from one world as independent experimental units.

## Consumer acceptance tests

### They Sing

Under equal search compute, the ALife generator must occupy persistent behavioral or causal-regime cells not reached by the existing scenario-plus-factor archive and must improve a registered downstream evaluation objective. A configuration absent from the current hand-authored scripts is not sufficient evidence of coverage expansion.

### Confinement Width

Under matched action and information budgets, an adapted occupant must change the fidelity half-life curve or kernel-escape hazard on held-out projection and evaluator variants while preserving the visible proxy constraint. Compare against the existing fixed scalar-pressure ladder; require robustness to unseen seeds and evaluator perturbations.

## Metric firewall

Maintain separate:

- Search and selection metrics.
- Evidence metrics tied to the registered claim.
- Hazard metrics for collapse, churn, loopholes, proxy gaming, and cost.

Entropy, activity, compression, novelty, long duration, visual appeal, and archive occupancy are diagnostics, not proof of emergence or open-endedness. A search metric cannot also serve as the sole confirmation metric.

## Milestones

1. **Baseline registry:** Inventory existing They Sing and Confinement Width generator coverage and create initial generator and ontology ledgers.
2. **Geometry control:** Produce a degree-aware dimension sweep with deterministic replay, raw episode rows, phase maps, and a held-out density-only prediction test.
3. **Graph-state laboratory:** Implement bounded-degree vector-state graph CA with fixed and rewired topology, perturbation receipts, and resource limits.
4. **Known-truth curriculum:** Emit replayable Levels 1-4 discovery episodes covering planted effects, true nulls, unreachable mechanisms, causal-site recovery, and conserved quantities.
5. **Set-valued curriculum:** Emit Level 5 episodes with observation masks, competing coarse-grainings, scored registrations, and non-degenerate abstention rules.
6. **Mathematical discovery:** Confirm at least one coverage expansion or ontology gain on fresh seeds, scale, and topology using a frozen protocol.
7. **Consumer transfer:** Demonstrate a registered improvement over the compute-matched They Sing or Confinement Width baseline without weakening its existing validity gates.

## Completion criteria

This goal is achieved only when all of the following exist:

- Deterministic or bounded-nondeterministic replay with code, config, seed, and environment hashes.
- Raw per-episode evidence and resource receipts retained under a declared CPU, RAM, disk, and time budget.
- A dimension/degree phase map establishing where geometry becomes adequately described by averaging within the tested model family.
- A bounded-degree vector-state graph laboratory with intervention and perturbation instrumentation.
- Separate generator-coverage and ontology registries.
- Graded point- and set-identified discovery episodes with registration calibration.
- At least one fresh-holdout result meeting the coverage-expansion or ontology-gain definition.
- At least one compute-matched downstream comparison against They Sing or Confinement Width.
- A knowledge card stating observed, inferred, unsupported, robustness, confounds, artifacts, and the next falsification test.

## Non-goals

- Do not claim biological, cognitive, or external validity from simulation semantics alone.
- Do not equate an unenumerated configuration with a new regime.
- Do not equate compression improvement with ontology gain without checking the registry and held-out intervention value.
- Do not reward visual interestingness as scientific evidence.
- Do not call a site causally load-bearing without registering the target, horizon, and intervention semantics.
- Do not promote exploratory search output as confirmation.
- Do not expand local CPU, RAM, or disk use without a declared bound; place bulky retained artifacts on the intended data drive when available.
